## Top News

**1. Google Workers Seek "Red Lines" on Military AI — Echoing Anthropic**
Google employees are formally petitioning leadership to establish hard limits on military AI contracts, citing autonomous weapons and mass surveillance as non-negotiable red lines. The petition explicitly references Anthropic's Pentagon standoff as a benchmark. With 254 HN points and 124 comments, this is the most-discussed AI story of the day.

**2. Anthropic CEO Refuses Pentagon's Demands**
Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei stated publicly that the company cannot accept Pentagon contract terms that would require removing Claude's safety guardrails — specifically around surveillance capabilities and autonomous weapons decision-making. This puts Anthropic in direct conflict with the DoD, one day after the company softened its own Responsible Scaling Policy.

**3. Palantir's AI Is Tracking Gaza Aid Deliveries**
Palantir's AI platform is being used by the U.S. government to track humanitarian aid logistics in Gaza, raising immediate questions about dual-use — the same infrastructure used for aid tracking is the same infrastructure used for military targeting. 139 HN points, 53 comments.

**4. Meta Signs Multi-Year Deal for Google TPUs**
Meta has signed a multi-year agreement to rent Google's TPU chips, marking the first time a major hyperscaler has publicly diversified away from near-exclusive Nvidia dependency. Signal: even companies that can buy whatever they want are hedging on Nvidia supply.

**5. Trump SOTU Mentions AI Twice**
The State of the Union addressed AI only twice and in passing, leaving the regulatory landscape as fragmented as it was entering 2026. No federal framework is coming soon; compliance complexity continues to fall on states and companies.

**Today's Signal:** The dominant story isn't capability — it's the collision between AI's military ambitions and the humans inside labs who built the systems. Yesterday was about whether safety pledges survive competitive pressure. Today is about whether they survive government pressure. The answer, so far, is: barely, and only at some companies.

## What Builders Are Talking About

**1. NanoClaw vs. OpenClaw: 500 Lines vs. 50,000**
NanoClaw — a lightweight alternative to OpenClaw built on Anthropic's Agents SDK — hit 600+ GitHub stars within 48 hours of launch and landed on the HN front page. The pitch: the entire codebase fits in a single reading session, uses OS-level container isolation instead of application-level permissions, and supports WhatsApp and Telegram natively. The community's reaction is enthusiastic and pointed — builders want AI agents they can actually audit.

**2. Implementing a Z80 / ZX Spectrum Emulator with Claude Code**
A developer used Claude Code to build a cycle-accurate Z80 emulator from a clean-room spec — no reference to existing emulator code. The thread is generating interest less for the emulator itself and more for what it reveals about Claude Code's ability to do low-level, specification-driven engineering work. 42 HN points, 41 comments.

**3. Parakeet.cpp — On-Device ASR in Pure C++ with Metal**
A new library for running Parakeet ASR (automatic speech recognition) inference in pure C++ with Metal GPU acceleration on Apple Silicon is gaining quiet traction. 88 HN points. Relevant to anyone building voice-native, on-device AI applications — no cloud, no latency, no API costs.

**Today's Signal:** The builder community's appetite for minimal, auditable, on-device tooling is accelerating. NanoClaw, Parakeet.cpp, and the Z80 emulator thread all share a common thread: developers want to understand the systems they're deploying. The unmet need is a coherent ecosystem of composable, auditable AI primitives — not more opaque mega-agents.

## Funding & Launches

**Runway raises $315M Series E at $5.3B valuation** (Feb 10, still the week's most significant round)
Runway's raise follows the release of Gen 4.5, which adds native audio, long-form multi-shot generation, and character consistency. Runway is positioning itself as the foundation model layer for AI video — not a tool, a platform.

**Encord closes $60M Series C** — Data infrastructure for robotics, total funding now $110M. Robotics data labeling and curation is emerging as a quiet but critical bottleneck as embodied AI moves toward deployment.

**Union.ai raises $38.1M Series A** (NEA-led) — Flyte, Union's open-source ML workflow platform, is benefiting from enterprises trying to orchestrate increasingly complex multi-step AI pipelines.

**Today's Signal:** Capital is flowing to the picks-and-shovels layer of two emerging stacks: AI video (Runway) and physical AI/robotics (Encord, Wayve). Application-layer bets are getting harder to make as foundation model providers keep moving down the stack. The safer investment thesis right now is infrastructure that every player needs regardless of which model wins.

## Discussions & Debates

**1. Google Workers' "Red Lines" Petition (254 HN points)**
The HN thread is a continuation of yesterday's Anthropic safety pledge debate, now applied to Google's situation. The core argument: if employees at the most capable AI labs are the primary check on military misuse, that's not a governance system — that's a prayer. Several comments draw a direct line between Anthropic's pledge reversal and the question of whether any internal ethics commitment is durable under external pressure.

**2. Palantir Tracking Gaza Aid (139 HN points)**
The thread is less about Palantir specifically and more about dual-use AI infrastructure — the same data pipelines, models, and APIs that track humanitarian logistics also power military targeting. The debate: is "we're only doing aid tracking" a meaningful distinction when the capability is the same? No consensus, but the discomfort is widespread.

**Today's Signal:** The AI community's anxiety today is about institutional accountability — not model capability. The question isn't "can AI do this?" but "who decides when it should?" The uncomfortable answer emerging across multiple threads: right now, individual employees and CEOs, with no durable external check in sight.
