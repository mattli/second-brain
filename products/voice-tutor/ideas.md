## 2026-07-23 — Steering brainstorm (pre-therapy, mobile)

**Session-opening outline.** At session start the tutor speaks a lay-of-the-land derived from the claim map — cluster claims into 2-4 topics (possibly one extra grouping field emitted at extraction) and open with "this doc is really about X, Y, Z — where do you want to start?" Triple duty: orients the session, hands the user the first steering move, and delivers input coverage of the map's shape so later elicitation feels fair. Also gives multi-session resumption a natural form ("last time you covered X; pick up at Y?").

**mark_claim tool — coverage as state, not vibes.** A tool call the tutor makes during conversation (claim id + mentioned vs articulated) so coverage survives long sessions and session boundaries. Recognized as the scoring foundation arriving through the steering door. Design wrinkle: live judgment happens with the tutor's attention split between conversing and judging → two-tier coverage: generous live marks (mentioned/discussed, enough to steer) + strict articulated-or-not call made post-session where scoring was always going to live.

**Secondary judge agent — evaluator blindness relocated into the product.** The tutor is a sighted, invested judge of "did he articulate that?" (it just taught the claim, wants the session flowing, grades its own conversation partner generously). A separate judge sees only transcript + claim map: no stake, no split attention, off the latency hot path. Verdict: right architecture, wrong time — v0 is tutor-marks-live + post-session strict judge (which IS a secondary judge, just async). A live parallel judge waits for a trigger: evidence that steering needs strict coverage mid-session. Whoever judges, verdicts must cite WHICH TURN constituted articulation — coverage anchors into the transcript exactly as claims anchor into the doc; likely the design seed for scoring.

**Elicitation-shape skepticism (Matt's flag).** "Explain that back to me" has a teacher-smell that could grate ×15/session. Gentler shapes that force articulation without recital: application ("what would the 1-1-1 lens say about your next project?"), prediction ("before I tell you what Harvey did — guess"), contrast ("how is that different from Cursor's move?"), flipped role (tutor plays skeptic, user defends the doc's argument). Explain-it-back is the fallback move, not the signature; the tutor's craft is matching elicitation shape to claim type (facts→contrast, frameworks→application, narratives→prediction). This is the make-or-break UX question — tune by ear.

**Reading mode / Readwise bleed — resolved.** No reader in Voice Tutor: reading is WikiTutor's/Readwise's lane; the claim map is the shared engine (cleanest answer yet to the one-engine question — WikiTutor serves docs-you-read, Voice Tutor serves docs-you-don't). Reading and tutoring are the two halves of the input/output coverage split: if the user has read the doc, the session's mix shifts teach-heavy → elicit-heavy (a claim-map parameter, not a new surface). Differentiation line: Readwise answers "what have I saved and read?"; Voice Tutor answers "what do I actually understand?" Bleed tell to watch in the roadmap: any feature making reading nicer rather than making articulation happen. Shelf item: during a session, glance-at-source on screen — surface the passage behind the current claim via the anchor offsets (the anchors earning their keep in UI; cousin of the recap browser).

**Conversation synthesis — the framing.** Not the audiobook version of reading: an audiobook is the same artifact in another channel — fixed, one-directional, attention optional. This synthesizes a conversation from the text: adaptive (rendering shifts with what the user knows and asks), bidirectional (the user's responses are part of the rendering), and elicitation lives inside the translation — the user produces half the expression. Category progression: TTS synthesizes speech; audiobooks perform text; podcasts discuss near you; this synthesizes a dialogue with you in it. Learning-science backbone: generation effect — retention follows production, and every prior format optimizes reception while this manufactures it. The claim map is the score the conversation is synthesized from — same doc, different performance every session and user.

**Wedge for user #1.** The missing consumption layer for documents never meant to be read: the pipeline compiles knowledge into the vault faster than any interface returns it to Matt — Voice Tutor is the return path, the read head for a knowledge base too dense to read (wiki pages are compiled for density, not readability; the awkwardness is the format doing its job). First audience hypothesis: PKM/Readwise/second-brain people whose systems outrun their retention ("I save everything and remember nothing") — findable by name, 1-1-1-shaped.

**Positioning line candidate:** not "replaces reading" — "some documents are for reading; yours are for knowing. This turns them into conversations you can actually have."

**NotebookLM differentiation:** audio overviews are a podcast ABOUT your doc — two hosts perform, you listen; still reception. This is the version where you're a participant and the system knows whether you could hold your end.

**v0 sequencing implied by all of the above:** wire claims into the study prompt (evening-sized, unblocked post-merge) → tutor prompt v1 drafted 2026-07-23 (teach → converse → elicit → move loop; private map never revealed; short voice turns) → tune elicitation by ear across sessions → mark_claim tool when vibes-coverage visibly fails → post-session strict judge = scoring's front door. Outline-opener is cheap and can ride the first wiring pass or the second.
