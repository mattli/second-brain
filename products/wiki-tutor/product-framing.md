# Wiki Tutor — Product Framing

*Working document. As of 2026-04-29.*

---

## What it is

An AI study companion that helps you learn from documents, with persistent memory across sessions. Users upload a document, highlight to ask questions, and receive a session artifact that gets saved to their vault. Over time, the system builds a knowledge graph of what they've studied.

---

## Differentiation vs. existing tools

- **vs. Explainpaper**: Has memory, vault integration, session artifacts, works on any document (not just research papers)
- **vs. Speechify**: Tutoring intent vs. accessibility/TTS; persistence vs. ephemeral
- **vs. Adobe AI Assistant**: Personal substrate (your vault) vs. generic doc Q&A
- **vs. Glasp**: Active tutoring loop vs. passive social highlighting

---

## One-line pitch

*"Explainpaper, but with memory and vault integration, for any document."*

---

## Two possible product framings (pick one to anchor)

- **Sells the moment**: "Understand this document faster"
- **Sells the relationship**: "Learn from everything you read, building knowledge over time"

The second is the wiki tutor framing and is the one to lean into.

---

## What the product really is (the deeper version)

Not just "an AI that knows what you've read" (fine, useful), but "an AI that knows how you think and how your understanding has evolved." The second is what makes this a tutor and not just a smarter search engine over your library.

---

## Voice: deferred

Originally framed as the defining feature. Now treated as a v2 feature, not core to MVP.

**Why deferred:**
- Speechify has 55M users — competing on voice/TTS quality is a losing battle
- Explainpaper validates that text-only highlight + Q&A works
- Real differentiation (persistence + vault + tutoring loop) doesn't require voice

**Self-check**: After text MVP works, will I actually add voice, or quietly drop it? If the latter, the deferral was avoidance, not strategy. Worth checking honestly.
