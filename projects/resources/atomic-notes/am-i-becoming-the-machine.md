"Am I becoming the machine or is the machine becoming me?"

I keep circling this question. I'm building a system that manages my to-do list, sends me briefings, stores my ideas, tracks what I've read, schedules my meals, reminds me of birthdays, and writes my tweets. It is both casually and profoundly changing the way I manage my life.

The casual part: it just handles things. The profound part: I'm externalizing cognition in a way that's hard to reverse. My morning routine is now: read the briefing the system generated, check the to-do list I dictated to it last night, ask it questions about what I read. The system is shaping my days as much as I'm shaping it.

This isn't necessarily bad. But it's worth noticing. The question isn't whether AI tools change how you think — they obviously do. The question is whether you're choosing the changes or just drifting into them.
