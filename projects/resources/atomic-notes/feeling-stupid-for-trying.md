I feel insecure about posting what I'm working on because everyone else seems way ahead of me. I have this constant worry that the things I'm building don't actually require the tech I'm using. Someone made fun of my .md project on Reddit and it almost sent me spiraling.

The deeper thing is I keep trying to remind myself what I'm building and I can't fully articulate it. "A system to help me research, validate, and build" sounds great but the process is constant experimentation and fine-tuning. I don't really know what it is yet. That ambiguity is uncomfortable, especially when I see others shipping polished products.

But the hedging-bets framing helped: if the product works, run it as indie. If it doesn't take off, I still have a shipped product in a space YC validated as important. Regardless of outcomes, the system itself keeps getting better because I used it to build something real. Every outcome moves forward.

The question I keep coming back to: why do I put so much pressure on myself? Why do I work so hard? I'm not one of these hustle culture guys. I work hard but I don't want to burn out. The answer I'm landing on is that the pressure comes from comparing my pace to other people's, when the real measure should be whether I'm learning and making progress on things I actually care about.
