I play guitar in the morning because it keeps me present. It's a good start to the day — something that engages your ability to listen, to think, and to keep your eyes open. After all, you just spent eight hours with your eyes closed.

Alan Watts said the best way to get into a meditative state is to listen. I've noticed this myself in meditation and sensory deprivation tanks. When you come out of a meditative state, I've always described it as "you hear the silence" — which is probably just another way to say the chatter has gone or subsided.

Reading can get you into a focused state too, but it feels wrong to go into another world as soon as you've come out of the subconscious world of sleep. Not to mention the research about morning sunlight. Playing guitar is physical, sonic, present-tense. It's the opposite of scrolling or reading news.

I pick up the guitar throughout the day, but the morning session is the one that matters. It's not practice in the deliberate sense — it's a way of arriving in the day.
