I can't brainstorm product ideas on demand. "List ten frictions in your life" produces silence for me because my life is mostly working. The advice doesn't land for people whose problems aren't dramatic enough to be obvious.

Ideas come from noticing moments, not from structured exercises. The Artemis 2 thing came up in therapy. The guitar learning autopilot came from frustration with TrueFire. The "wiki asks you questions" idea came from staring at my own wiki and feeling nothing. The .md project came from watching how AI instruction files were being managed across tools.

Tech-first ideation is a trap. "What can I build with this cool stack?" leads nowhere durable. The job is picking a person and an outcome, not predicting the platform layer. The best ideas I've had came from being deep in a domain and noticing something that didn't work the way it should.

The practical implication: I need to stop scheduling "brainstorm product ideas" as a to-do item and instead build the habit of writing down moments of friction as they happen. The inbox pattern — dump it in, process it later — is the right shape for capturing ideas. The wrong shape is sitting down and demanding creativity from myself.
