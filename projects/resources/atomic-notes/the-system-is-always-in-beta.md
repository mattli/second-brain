One recurring stress is that I'm continually trying to remind myself what it is I'm building. Every time I settle on a workflow, I find something that doesn't work and have to tweak it again. The briefing sources need adjusting. The to-do list format keeps evolving. The product ideas shift. It never feels done.

I had to reframe the whole thing as still being in a testing phase. That reframe was necessary for my sanity. The system will never be finished because the system is me — my priorities change, my understanding deepens, my needs evolve.

This is what finding product-market fit feels like when you're the market. The product is molding around my own use cases and it's about feeling my way through it, feeling and understanding what's effective and what's not. That requires constant experimentation and allowing the system to evolve.

The lesson I keep having to re-learn: the instability IS the process. It's not a sign that something is wrong. It's a sign that I'm actually using the system seriously enough for its limitations to surface. The alternative — a perfectly configured system I never touch — would mean I stopped caring.
