I have about two to three hours of serious AI/building work in me per day. I saw someone else mention this online and it resonated. It's mentally fatiguing in a way that's different from other kinds of work. It's not just when the laptop is open — there's going on X and looking at stuff, saving stuff, brainstorming, taking notes. I'm also trying to learn and read. And I'm by myself.

I keep beating myself up for not doing enough. I didn't read Infinite Jest today. I didn't stretch. But I was basically trying to be productive all day. The honest answer is that a lot of this work is invisible — the thinking, the research, the half-formed ideas that don't have a checkbox.

I need to stop measuring days by how many items I checked off and start measuring them by whether I moved something forward, even a little. The to-do list is a guide, not a verdict. Some days I'll do more, some days less. The pressure I put on myself to optimize every hour is itself a source of burnout.
