In a future where we won't know what's real — actually human in videos, audio, or text — will people accept AI-generated content as a substitute, or will they crave something that is genuinely human? Is the human experience replaceable?

I don't have a clear answer, but I notice the question keeps pulling at me. When I post a tweet that my AI helped me write, is that still mine? When the briefing system writes a summary of the week's AI news, is that research or is that outsourcing my understanding?

The deeper version of this question showed up when I was at the Ratboys show at the Terragram. Standing there alone, anxious, drinking a beer I don't even drink. That was irreducibly human. No system could have generated that experience or understood why it mattered. The music, the ringing ears, the bittersweet feeling of starting new — those are the things that can't be automated.

Maybe the answer is that AI handles the information layer (briefings, summaries, scheduling) and frees up space for the experience layer (concerts, guitar, reading, writing). The danger is when the information layer starts to feel like the whole thing.
