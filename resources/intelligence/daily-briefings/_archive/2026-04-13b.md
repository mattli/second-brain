# AI Intelligence Briefing — 2026-04-13

## Top News

**Kepler's orbital compute cluster is open for business.** Kepler Communications, a Canadian startup, announced today that its cluster of 40 Nvidia Orin edge processors across 10 satellites — linked by laser communications — now has 18 customers. Its newest, Sophia Space, will attempt the first-ever orbital software deployment across 6 GPUs on 2 spacecraft. The experiment is table stakes for a terrestrial data center; in orbit, it's never been done. Meanwhile, a Wisconsin data center construction ban and congressional pressure toward similar restrictions are, per Sophia's CEO, making the space-based alternative look more attractive by the week.

**U.S. regulators push banks to use Anthropic's Mythos — while the DoD still considers Anthropic a supply-chain risk.** Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent and Federal Reserve Chair Jerome Powell separately met with bank executives this week to encourage testing of Claude Mythos for vulnerability detection. Goldman Sachs, Citigroup, Bank of America, and Morgan Stanley are reportedly now testing the model. The contradiction is sharp: the same administration whose Pentagon designated Anthropic a supply-chain risk in March is now directing Wall Street to adopt its most powerful, restricted model. U.K. financial regulators are separately convening to discuss the risks Mythos poses.

**At HumanX, the enterprise AI world had moved on from OpenAI — to Claude.** At the HumanX conference in San Francisco this week, TechCrunch's reporter found that Claude was the dominant model being discussed by both panelists and vendors, while ChatGPT was notably absent from most conversations. One vendor described OpenAI as having "fallen off." The backdrop: a New Yorker profile questioning Sam Altman's trustworthiness, the shutdown of Sora and ChatGPT's "erotic mode," and OpenAI's advertising pivot. OpenAI responded with a $100/month ChatGPT Pro tier giving more access to Codex — a direct shot at Claude Code's developer traction.

**Vercel CEO signals IPO readiness as AI agent revenue surges.** At HumanX, Vercel CEO Guillermo Rauch said "The company is ready and getting more ready every day" for an IPO, citing a significant revenue surge driven by AI agent workloads running on its infrastructure.

**Claude.ai is down this morning.** An ongoing outage at Claude.ai is being tracked at status.claude.com and generating discussion on Hacker News. No estimated resolution time available at time of writing.

**Today's Signal:** The Mythos banking story is the week's most consequential development — the U.S. government is actively deploying frontier AI for defensive cybersecurity while simultaneously litigating against the lab that made it. This isn't incoherence; it's the first glimpse of a post-trust AI policy: use the capabilities, don't trust the company. Combined with the Claude-dominates-HumanX story, the pattern is clear: Anthropic is winning the enterprise and the policy rooms simultaneously.

## Funding & Product News

**OpenAI launches $100/month ChatGPT Pro tier with expanded Codex access.** Announced this week at HumanX, the new plan is explicitly designed to convert Claude Code users back to OpenAI's ecosystem. It gives significantly more access to Codex, OpenAI's coding tool.

The infrastructure layer (Vercel eyeing IPO, orbital compute going commercial) is quietly becoming the story beneath the model wars.

## Discussions & Debates

**"The Future of Everything is Lies, I Guess: Safety" — Aphyr's comprehensive takedown of AI alignment.** Software engineer Kyle Kingsbury (aphyr) published a long, technically detailed essay today arguing that LLM alignment is fundamentally inadequate: that it is expensive and optional, that anyone can train an unaligned model by skipping it, that current safeguards fail regularly at scale, and that the conditions are being created for malicious actors to clone capability without safety work. The essay goes further — cataloguing AI-enabled fraud, automated harassment, CSAM generation, and the use of Palantir's Claude-integrated system in targeting decisions during U.S. strikes in Iran (which it links to a school attack killing children). The author writes from the position of a Mastodon moderator who reviews AI-generated CSAM as a legal obligation. It is gaining early traction on HN.

**Missouri fires half its city council over a data center deal.** A Politico story trending on HN today covers a Missouri town that removed half its city council following a controversial data center deal residents opposed. Separately, Wisconsin passed a data center construction ban this week. Local political backlash against data center siting is beginning to produce actual democratic consequences.

**Today's Signal:** Both debates are about who controls the physical and epistemic infrastructure of AI. One community is using elections to fight the power grids and zoning decisions that AI requires. Another is arguing that the safety promises labs make to justify those power grids and data centers are marketing copy. These aren't separate debates — they're the same one.
